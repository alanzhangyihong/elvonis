**One failed cleaning inspection. An entire production batch recalled.**

In food manufacturing and pharmaceutical cleanrooms, cleaning isn't just about removing visible dirt.

It's about product safety, regulatory compliance, brand integrity — and in healthcare, patient lives.

Here's exactly what these high-standard environments require from their cleaning equipment.

 

## **The Unique Challenges of Food Plant & Cleanroom Cleaning**

Standard commercial cleaning equipment can actually become a contamination source in food facilities.

**Core challenges:**

✗ Standard equipment gaps and materials harbor bacteria, forming biofilms

✗ Residual detergents can contaminate food products

✗ Non-explosion-proof equipment poses detonation risk in dust environments (flour, sugar, starch)

✗ Standard vacuums cannot achieve cleanroom-required filtration precision

✗ Metal component detachment can compromise metal detection quality control

## **Equipment Selection by Facility Zone**

**Production Floor (Meat / Dairy / Ready-to-Eat)**

Contamination: fat, protein residue, blood, warm humid conditions.

Recommended: Hot water floor scrubber (70°C+ sterilization cleaning) + HACCP-compliant pressure washer.

Requirements: Stainless steel components, food-grade seals, IP55+ waterproofing.

**Dry Ingredient Zones (Flour / Sugar / Starch / Grain)**

Contamination: airborne dust, explosion risk.

Recommended: ATEX-certified explosion-proof industrial vacuum (Class M/H).

Requirements: Spark-free components, anti-static design, mandatory grounding connection.

**Packaging & Filling Lines**

Contamination: packaging debris, product spillage, label adhesive residue.

Recommended: Compact walk-behind scrubber + cordless industrial vacuum (for operational flexibility).

Requirements: Low noise to avoid production disruption; fast-drying to prevent slip hazards.

**Pharmaceutical Cleanrooms (ISO Class 7/8)**

Contamination: airborne particles, microbial contamination.

Recommended: HEPA H14 industrial vacuum + dedicated cleanroom floor scrubber.

Requirements: Zero secondary particle generation; materials compliant with cleanroom specs (no shedding, no corrosion).

**Raw Material Warehouse / Cold Storage**

Contamination: packaging dust, forklift tire deposits, ice accumulation.

Recommended: Low-temperature industrial vacuum (cold-storage rated) + walk-behind scrubber.

Requirements: Equipment must operate reliably in 0°C-5°C environments.

## **Core Standards for GMP/HACCP-Compliant Cleaning Equipment**

**Material Requirements:**

All surfaces that contact or may contact food must use food-grade stainless steel (316L) or food-grade plastic.

Prohibited: easily-flaking coatings, standard rubber (contains PAHs).

**IP Waterproofing Rating:**

Production area equipment: minimum IP55. Rinse-down zones: IP65 or higher.

**Filtration Precision:**

Cleanroom and pharmaceutical vacuums must be equipped with HEPA H13/H14 filters rated ≥99.95% efficiency.

**Explosion-Proof Certification:**

Dust explosion hazard zones (Zone 21/22) require ATEX or IECEx certified equipment — no exceptions.

**Cleaning Validation Documentation:**

Opt for equipment that generates digital cleaning records and operation reports to support audit requirements.

## **How to Verify Equipment Compliance**

Request from suppliers: CE certification, FDA compliance documentation (for US export), ATEX certification (explosion-proof), ISO 9001 quality management certification.

Ask for reference cases from comparable food manufacturing facilities.

Conduct a pilot trial before full deployment; validate using ATP bioluminescence testing.

## **Critical Purchasing Mistakes to Avoid**

**Don't cut costs with standard commercial models**

Non-compliant equipment creates regulatory risk. One failed audit costs far more than the price difference between compliant and standard equipment.

**Evaluate how easy the equipment itself is to clean**

Equipment must be regularly sanitized. Complex structures with hidden crevices become hygiene liabilities.

**Implement color-coded equipment zoning**

Strict zone-specific use (color-coded by area) prevents cross-contamination between production areas.

## **Final Thoughts**

Food safety has zero margin for error. Cleaning equipment selection is a foundational element of the entire food safety system.

Compliant equipment protects consumers — and protects your business from regulatory, financial, and reputational risk.

**一次清洁不达标，可能导致整批产品召回。**

食品工厂和医药洁净室的清洁，不只是「把地面洗干净」这么简单。

它关系到产品安全、法规合规、企业信誉，甚至是患者生命。

本文告诉你：这些高标准场所到底需要什么样的清洁设备。

 

## **一、食品工厂和洁净室清洁的特殊挑战**

普通商用清洁设备进入食品工厂，可能反而成为污染源。

**核心挑战：**

✗ 普通设备的缝隙和材质容易藏菌，形成生物膜

✗ 残留清洁剂可能污染产品

✗ 非防爆设备在粉尘环境中存在爆炸风险（面粉、糖粉、淀粉）

✗ 普通吸尘器无法达到洁净室所需的过滤精度

✗ 金属零件脱落（金属检测品控关卡）

## **二、食品工厂各区域清洁设备选型**

**生产车间（肉类 / 乳制品 / 熟食）**

污染类型：脂肪、蛋白质残留、血水、温热潮湿环境。

推荐设备：热水洗地机（70℃以上热水灭菌清洁）+ HACCP合规高压清洗机。

关键要求：不锈钢部件、食品级密封材料、防水IP55以上等级。

**干燥物料区（面粉 / 糖 / 淀粉 / 谷物）**

污染类型：粉尘飞扬、爆炸风险。

推荐设备：ATEX认证防爆工业吸尘器（M/H级别）。

关键要求：无火花元件、防静电设计、接地连接。

**包装车间 / 灌装线**

污染类型：包装碎料、产品溢出、标签残胶。

推荐设备：小型手推洗地机 + 无绳工业吸尘器（灵活操作）。

关键要求：低噪音不干扰生产线，快速干燥防滑倒。

**医药洁净室（ISO 7 / ISO 8级别）**

污染类型：颗粒物、微生物污染。

推荐设备：HEPA H14级工业吸尘器 + 专用洁净室洗地机。

关键要求：不产生次生颗粒、材质符合洁净室规范（无脱粉、无腐蚀）。

**原材料仓库 / 冷库**

污染类型：包装尘、叉车轮胎碳黑、冷库积冰。

推荐设备：低温型工业吸尘器（适合冷库环境）+ 手推洗地机。

关键要求：设备可在0℃-5℃低温环境正常运行。

## **三、符合GMP/HACCP的清洁设备核心标准**

**材质要求：**

设备与食品接触或可能接触的部分，必须采用食品级不锈钢（316L）或食品级塑料。

禁止使用容易脱落的涂层、普通橡胶（含多环芳烃）。

**防水等级：**

生产车间清洁设备防水等级应达到IP55以上，冲洗区域需IP65或以上。

**过滤精度：**

洁净室和药厂用吸尘器必须配备HEPA H13/H14滤芯，过滤效率≥99.95%。

**防爆认证：**

粉尘爆炸危险区域（Zone 21/22）必须使用经ATEX或IECEx认证的防爆设备。

**清洁验证文档：**

建议选购可提供清洁记录、作业报告的设备（支持电子记录便于审计）。

## **四、如何验证清洁设备的合规性？**

要求供应商提供：CE认证、FDA合规证明（出口美国）、ATEX认证（防爆要求）、ISO 9001质量管理认证。

向供应商索取同类食品工厂的使用案例和验证报告。

建议引入前进行小批量试用，配合内部卫生验证（ATP荧光检测）。

## **五、食品工厂清洁设备采购注意事项**

**不要贪便宜买普通商用型号**

非食品级设备长期使用会带来合规风险，一次审计不通过的代价远超设备差价。

**关注设备清洁便利性**

设备本身也需要定期清洗消毒，结构复杂、死角多的设备会成为卫生隐患。

**建立清洁设备专用标识**

不同区域的清洁设备严格分区使用（颜色编码管理），防止交叉污染。
