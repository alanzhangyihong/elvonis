A biscuit factory had three cleaning shifts per day with complete signed records covering equipment, floors, and walls. Three consecutive customer audits found the same dust non-conformance in the same equipment zone. Three rectification cycles, each two to three weeks of reduced output. Total disruption losses exceeded the entire annual cleaning budget.

Complete records, full shifts. The problem remained.

Four responses were tried in sequence: cleaning frequency increased to three times daily; additional dedicated cleaning staff added; HEPA filter grade upgraded; higher-power vacuum equipment purchased.

The next audit found the same finding in the same location.

If increased frequency, more staff, and better equipment all failed, the problem may not be a cleaning problem.

So why does dust keep returning to the same surfaces despite daily cleaning?

Food factories run ventilation at more than ten air changes per hour - a food safety regulatory requirement.

Air moves through the facility in a defined circuit: in through supply air diffusers, across the production area, out through return vents.

That moving air carries suspended particles, typically one to one hundred microns in size, which can stay airborne for hours in moving air.

When airflow reaches the terminal points of its circuit and slows, those particles settle onto the nearest horizontal surfaces.

Equipment tops, cable trays, and light fixture tops all sit at these terminal airflow positions.

Cleaning removes what has already settled. The ventilation system keeps running and delivers fresh particles back to the exact same locations.

This is not a problem of too much dust being generated. It is a continuously operating particle transport system delivering particles to fixed terminal positions.

Assess three locations: the equipment top with heaviest accumulation, cable trays in the production area, and horizontal surfaces directly below supply air diffusers.

Observe two indicators: whether dust distribution pattern aligns with supply air direction; how quickly visible dust reappears after cleaning.

Record one phenomenon: whether past audit findings consistently appear on the same set of horizontal surfaces.

If all three confirm the pattern, airflow is the primary particle transport pathway.

The solution sequence starts at the source: first, identify major dust generation points in the facility; second, install local exhaust ventilation at high-generation points to capture particles before they enter the general airflow; third, review the ventilation circuit and consider whether supply air diffuser positions can reduce particle delivery to high-risk zones; fourth, only after completing the first three steps, re-evaluate cleaning frequency based on actual measured accumulation rates. Equipment is always the last step.

Facilities that systematically addressed airflow and source generation recorded: accumulation rate reductions of fifty to seventy percent at major deposition points after local exhaust installation; measurable decreases in repeat audit findings in the two subsequent audits; and cleaning labor reduction as frequency requirements decreased. Three simultaneous improvements, not from spending more, but from addressing the correct problem.

Airflow delivers particles to terminal horizontal surfaces. Which surface accumulates fastest?

The answer is almost always equipment tops.

Why specifically there? There is a physics reason most people have not considered.

一家饼干工厂，三班清洁，记录全部由主管签字确认，设备表面、地面、墙壁都纳入了清洁范围。

但这家工厂连续三次客户审核，审核员都在同一片设备区域发现积尘超标。

三次整改，每次停线两到三周，整改文件越写越厚，问题依然没有变化。

按日产值折算，三次整改的总损失超过了整个保洁团队的全年预算。

清洁记录这么完整，问题为什么还在那里？

工厂讨论之后，先后试了四个方向：把清洁频率从每天一次增加到每天三次；增加专职保洁人员数量；把原来的HEPA滤芯升级到更高等级；购买了功率更大的吸尘设备。

下一次审核，发现还在同样的位置，几乎没有变化。

如果增加频率、增加人员、升级设备都没有解决问题，那问题可能根本不在清洁本身。

那每天都在清洁，粉尘为什么还是反复出现在同样的地方？

食品工厂的换气系统，为了满足食品安全法规的要求，需要每小时进行十次以上的全空间换气。

空气在整个车间里沿着特定路径流动：从送风口进入，流过生产区，从回风口排出。

在流动的过程中，气流携带着大量悬浮颗粒，粒径通常在1到100微米之间，这个范围的颗粒在流动空气中可以保持悬浮数小时。

当气流到达路径末端、速度降低的时候，这些颗粒开始沉降，落在最近的水平面上。

设备顶部、桥架表面、灯具顶面，正好都处于气流路径的末端位置。

你清洁掉的是已经沉降在那里的颗粒；但换气系统还在运转，下一轮气流又把整个空间的悬浮颗粒重新输送到同样的末端位置。

问题不是粉尘产生太多，而是有一台持续运转的颗粒输送系统，在不停地把颗粒送到这些固定位置。

现场评估时，检查三个位置：积尘最明显的设备顶部、电缆桥架、送风口正下方区域的水平面。

观察两个指标：积尘分布方向是否和送风口的吹风方向一致；清洁完成之后多久重新出现可见积尘。

记录一个现象：过去所有审核发现的位置，是不是集中在固定的几个水平面上。

如果以上三点都成立，说明气流是主要的颗粒输送路径，清洁频率不是根本问题。

解决方案的顺序从源头开始：第一，排查车间内主要粉尘产生点；第二，在高产尘位置安装局部排风装置，在颗粒进入换气气流之前就捕集掉；第三，评估换气系统的气流路径，考虑是否需要调整送风口位置以减少颗粒向高风险区域的输送量；第四，在完成前三步之后，再重新评估清洁频率，以实际积尘速率为依据，而不是以"感觉应该清洁更多"为依据。设备采购永远是最后一步。

针对气流和源头进行系统性改善的工厂，记录显示：局部排风安装后主要沉降位置的积尘速率下降约50-70%；审核重复发现在改善后两次审核中有明显下降；清洁频率因积尘速率降低而减少，保洁成本同步下降。三个改善同时发生，不是因为投入更多，而是因为解决对了问题。

气流把颗粒送到末端水平面-那积尘速率最高的是哪个？

答案几乎在每个工厂都是一样的：设备顶部。
